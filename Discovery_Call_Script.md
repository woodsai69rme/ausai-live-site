# Discovery Call Script — AusAI Tech

> **Purpose:** Turn an interested reply into a qualified, closed deal in 15 minutes.
> **When to use:** After a prospect responds to LinkedIn DM, cold email, or Reddit outreach.
> **Prep time:** 2 minutes (scan their website + last 3 LinkedIn posts).
> **Target close rate:** 30-50% of discovery calls → proposal sent.

---

## Pre-Call Checklist (do not skip)

- [ ] Website scanned for 30 sec — note platform, obvious issues, scale signals
- [ ] LinkedIn profile reviewed — recent posts, job changes, mutual connections
- [ ] Call link sent (Calendly) with reminder 15 min before
- [ ] Proposal template + Invoice template open in adjacent tabs (ready to customise)
- [ ] Notepad ready for notes (you will reference these in follow-up)
- [ ] **MEDDIC quick-qualify** — mentally check: do they have **M**etrics (quantified pain), **E**conomic buyer (are they the decision maker?), **D**ecision criteria (what do they need to see?), **D**ecision process (how do they buy?), **I**dentify pain (do they feel it urgently?), **C**hampion (will they advocate for you?). If 3+ are missing, deprioritise this call.

---

## The 15-Minute Structure

| Phase | Time | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Rapport** | 0:00–1:00 | Lower guard, set agenda |
| **2. Pain** | 1:00–5:00 | Uncover specific pain + cost of inaction |
| **3. Value** | 5:00–9:00 | Bridge pain to your solution, get them to articulate value |
| **4. Close** | 9:00–13:00 | Propose next step, handle objections, get verbal yes |
| **5. Lock** | 13:00–15:00 | Confirm details, send proposal while on call |

---

## Phase 1: Rapport (0:00–1:00)

**Opening (smile — they can hear it):**
> "Thanks for jumping on, {first_name}. I know your time is tight, so I'll keep this to 15 minutes — does that still work for you?"

**Agenda set:**
> "Quick structure: I'll ask 3-4 questions about what's bugging you right now, then if it sounds like I can help, I'll tell you exactly how it works and what it costs. If not, no worries — you'll still get a couple of quick wins you can implement today. Sound fair?"

**Why this works:**
- You promised a hard stop (respects time).
- You offered free value either way (removes pressure).
- You got verbal "yes" to agenda (commitment consistency).

---

## Phase 2: Pain + Qualification (1:00–5:00)

Use **MEDDIC** to qualify fast. Industry top performers (40%+ close rate) strictly filter out tire-kickers before offering calls. Average discovery-call-to-close is 22-25%; your goal with strict qualification is 30%+.

**Question 1 — The hook (Metrics + Identify Pain):**
> "When you replied to my message, you mentioned {reference their reply}. Walk me through that — what's the actual situation right now?"

*Listen for: scope, urgency, who else is involved, budget authority. Quantify the pain in dollars or hours.*

**Question 2 — The cost of inaction (Metrics):**
> "If nothing changes in the next 30 days, what does that look like — in dollars, hours, or risk?"

*Listen for: revenue loss, time drain, compliance risk, team frustration. Write down the number they give you — you will use it in Phase 3.*

**Question 3 — Past attempts (Decision Criteria + Process):**
> "Have you tried to fix this before — either internally or with another provider? What happened?"

*Listen for: bad past experiences (position against them), DIY failures (urgency), budget already spent (capacity).*

**Question 4 — Decision process (Economic Buyer + Champion):**
> "If we decide this makes sense, who else needs to sign off — budget, legal, technical?"

*If not the decision maker:*
> "No problem at all. What's the best way to get them looped in — would a 1-page proposal they can skim work?"

*If they can't name the decision maker or process → yellow flag. Proceed but deprioritise emotionally.*

---

## Phase 3: Value (5:00–9:00)

**Bridge statement (summarise THEIR words back to them):**
> "Okay, so what I'm hearing is: {repeat their pain in their words}. And if that stays broken, you're looking at {cost of inaction they described}. Does that sound right?"

*Wait for "yes." This is the most important verbal commitment in the call.*

**Introduce the solution (one sentence):**
> "Here's how we fix that: {one-line description of your service}."

**Value-based pricing anchor (if you have their cost-of-inaction number):**
> "You mentioned this is costing you roughly ${their number}/month. Our engagement fee is ${your fee} — that's a {X}-month payback if we solve it."

*Formula: Price = Annual Value Created × 10-25%. If their pain costs $2k/month ($24k/year), a $2,400-6,000 fee is justified. State the payback period explicitly.*

**Social proof (15 seconds):**
> "I ran this exact process for {similar client type} last month. They were dealing with {similar pain}. We delivered in {timeframe}, and they said {specific result, ideally a quote}."

*If you have no client yet:*
> "I've built and stress-tested this process on {number} internal runs. The last test run found {specific impressive finding} in under 2 hours."

**The pivot question:**
> "Based on what you just told me, which piece is the most urgent for you — the {deliverable A}, the {deliverable B}, or getting the whole thing done end-to-end?"

*This gets them choosing BETWEEN options you offer, not WHETHER to buy.*

---

## Phase 4: Close (9:00–13:00)

**State the investment clearly:**
> "For the scope we just discussed, the investment is A$XXX. That includes {list 3 key inclusions}. Turnaround is {timeframe}."

**Then shut up.**

*First person to speak loses. Count to 5 in your head.*

> **Conversion benchmark context:** Average discovery-call-to-close for B2B consulting is 22-25%. Top performers hit 38% by using interactive demos or free samples during/after the call. If you're below 20% after 10 calls, tighten qualification (MEDDIC) or improve your social proof story.

---

### Objection Handling

#### "That's more than I expected"

> "I get that. Let me ask: is it the total number, or the timing of the cash out that's the issue?"

*If total:*
> "Fair. Let's look at what we can scope back. If we drop {deliverable} and focus just on {core deliverable}, that brings it to A$XXX. You can always add the rest later. Would that work?"

*If timing:*
> "No problem — I can split it: 50% to start, 50% on delivery. Or if cash flow is tight this month, we can start with the A$200 basic scan and upgrade once you see the value."

#### "I need to think about it"

> "Of course — smart to think before spending. To help you decide, what's the one thing you're unsure about?"

*Isolate the objection. Do not let them leave with a vague "maybe."*

> "If that one thing were resolved, would you move forward?"

*If yes → resolve it now. If no → there's a deeper objection. Ask:*
> "Got it. Is there something else holding you back — budget, timing, or not sure I'm the right person?"

#### "I need to ask my partner / boss / team"

> "Absolutely. What do they usually care most about — cost, timeline, or proof it works?"

*Then:*
> "I'll send you a 1-page proposal right now. It has the scope, timeline, and a sample of what the deliverable looks like. You can forward it straight to them. Can I send it to {email} while we're still on the call?"

*Send it during the call. Get them to confirm receipt.*

#### "Can you do a free trial / sample first?"

> "I don't do free custom work — it devalues the output for paying clients. But here's what I can do: I'll run a 5-minute surface scan on your site right now and tell you the top 2 things I'd look at first. No obligation. Want me to do that?"

*Run the scan live on the call. Show real findings. Then:*
> "That's just the surface. The full audit goes 10 layers deeper. Ready to book it in?"

#### "We already have someone for this"

> "That's great — means you're already investing in solving it. When did they last deliver results?"

*If >3 months ago:*
> "A lot changes in 3 months — new CVEs, plugin updates, config drift. A quick independent scan is cheap insurance."

*If recent:*
> "Fair enough. I'm not trying to replace them — I can be a second opinion. Most of my clients use me as a quarterly external audit to catch what internal teams miss. Worth a one-off check?"

---

## Phase 5: Lock (13:00–15:00)

**Verbal confirmation:**
> "Great — so we're agreed: {service tier} at A$XXX, {timeframe} delivery, starting {date}. I'll send the proposal and invoice now."

**Get the email address:**
> "What's the best email for the proposal and Stripe invoice?"

**Send while on call:**
- Open `Proposal_Template.html` → fill placeholders → print to PDF → attach.
- Open `Invoice_Template.html` → fill placeholders → print to PDF → attach OR send Stripe link.
- Email subject: "AusAI Tech — Proposal & Invoice: {Project Name}"

**Final close:**
> "Just sent it. Can you check your inbox and confirm you got it?"

*Wait for them to open it. This dramatically increases same-day close rate.*

> "Perfect. Once the deposit hits, I'll block out {date} on my calendar and send you the kickoff checklist. Any questions before we wrap?"

**Wrap:**
> "Awesome — excited to work with you, {first_name}. Chat soon."

## Red Flags — Disqualify Fast

Say no to these prospects. Your time is worth more than the revenue.

| Red flag | Why | Response |
|---|---|---|
| "We need this yesterday but can't pay till next quarter" | Cash flow problem becomes YOUR cash flow problem | "Let's reconnect next quarter" |
| "Can you do it for exposure / equity?" | Devalues your work; equity in unknown company is worthless | "I only work on paid engagements" |
| "Our last 3 freelancers ghosted us" | They are the common denominator | "I require 50% deposit upfront" |
| "We don't know what we want — just fix everything" | Scope creep guaranteed | "I need a defined scope to quote. Let's start with a A$200 discovery call." |
| "Can you copy {competitor} exactly?" | Ethical/legal risk; devalues your expertise | "I build custom solutions. If you want a clone, I'm not the right fit." |
| Multiple reschedules before first call | Low priority = low close rate | One more reschedule max, then deprioritise |

# Proposal Follow-Up System — AusAI Tech

> **Purpose:** Systematized multi-channel follow-up for sent proposals. Never let a proposal die in silence. Structured 14-day cadence with value-adds, not pestering.
> **When to use:** Immediately after sending any proposal. Follow the cadence until closed or disqualified.
> **Prerequisite:** `Proposal_Template.html`, `EMAIL_TEMPLATES.md`, `LEAD_TRACKER.html`

---

## The Follow-Up Philosophy

Most consultants send a proposal and wait. If the client doesn't respond, they send one awkward follow-up, then give up. **This is where revenue dies.**

The fix: a structured, value-adding cadence that gives the prospect a reason to reply at every touchpoint. You're not "checking in" — you're adding value each time.

**14-day cadence. 6 touchpoints. Multi-channel.**

---

## The 14-Day Cadence

| Day | Channel | What You Send | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Day 0** | Email | Proposal + invoice | Initial send (during or right after call) |
| **Day 2** | Email | Value-add #1: relevant case study or article | Keep top of mind + demonstrate expertise |
| **Day 5** | LinkedIn | Soft touch: "Saw this and thought of you" | Different channel, low pressure |
| **Day 9** | Email | Value-add #2: DIY tip or resource | Give value without asking for anything |
| **Day 12** | Email | Gentle nudge: "Any questions on the proposal?" | Direct ask, limited time pressure |
| **Day 14** | Email | Close-out: permission to archive | Walk-away close — triggers urgency |

---

## Day 0: Proposal Sent (During or After Call)

Already covered in `Discovery_Call_Script.md` Phase 5. Send proposal + invoice while on call if possible.

**If sent during call:**
> "Just sent it. Can you check your inbox and confirm you got it?"

**If sent after call:**
> **Subject:** AusAI Tech — Proposal: {Project Name}
>
> Hi {NAME},
>
> Great chat today. Here's the proposal we discussed.
>
> **Scope:** {1-line summary}
> **Investment:** A${amount}
> **Timeline:** {X} days from deposit
>
> Attached: proposal + invoice. Any questions, just reply.
>
> Best,
> Karma

---

## Day 2: Value-Add #1 (Email)

**Don't ask "any updates?"** — they'll ignore it. Instead, send something useful.

**Option A — Case study:**

> **Subject:** Thought you'd find this relevant
>
> Hi {NAME},
>
> Came across this case study from a similar project — thought you'd find it useful as you're evaluating options: [link to CASE_STUDY_TEMPLATE.md output or relevant article]
>
> No action needed — just wanted to share.
>
> Best,
> Karma

**Option B — Relevant resource:**

> **Subject:** Quick resource — {their industry} automation
>
> Hi {NAME},
>
> Saw this article on automation in {their industry} and thought of our conversation. The section on {specific topic} is especially relevant to what we discussed.
>
> [Link or summary]
>
> Cheers,
> Karma

---

## Day 5: LinkedIn Soft Touch

**Only if you're connected on LinkedIn.** Different channel, zero sales pressure.

> "Saw {article/post} about {their industry/interest} — reminded me of our chat last week. Hope the proposal was useful!"

**Don't mention the proposal directly.** This is a top-of-mind touch, not a follow-up.

---

## Day 9: Value-Add #2 (Email)

**Give them something free they can use today.** Positions you as a resource, not just a vendor.

> **Subject:** Quick automation tip (no strings)
>
> Hi {NAME},
>
> Quick tip related to {their pain point} that you can implement yourself today:
>
> {1-paragraph actionable tip — e.g., "Use Zapier's free tier to connect Form X to Slack. Takes 5 minutes. Here's how..."}
>
> No pitch — just thought it might save you some time while you're evaluating options.
>
> Best,
> Karma

---

## Day 12: Gentle Direct Nudge (Email)

**Now you can ask directly. It's been 12 days — you've added value twice. You've earned the ask.**

> **Subject:** Quick check — {Project Name} proposal
>
> Hi {NAME},
>
> Just checking in — any questions on the proposal I sent over? Happy to jump on a 5-minute call if anything needs clarifying.
>
> If the timing isn't right, no worries at all — just let me know and I'll stop following up.
>
> Best,
> Karma

---

## Day 14: The Close-Out (Email)

**The "permission to close your file" email.** This triggers urgency in serious prospects and gives non-serious ones a graceful exit.

> **Subject:** Closing the loop — {Project Name}
>
> Hi {NAME},
>
> I haven't heard back on the {Project Name} proposal, so I'm going to assume the timing isn't right and close this out on my end.
>
> No hard feelings at all — if things change or the need comes back around, just reply to this email. I'll pick it right back up.
>
> In the meantime, here's the proposal PDF for your files.
>
> Best,
> Karma

**Why this works:**
- "Permission to close your file" is a psychological trigger (loss aversion)
- Removes the pressure (they're free to say no)
- Leaves the door open (one reply restarts everything)
- Many non-responders reply to this email — often with "Sorry, busy — let's talk next week." The permission-to-archive framing creates urgency without pressure.

## After Day 14: Archive

- [ ] Move proposal to "Closed — No Response" in `LEAD_TRACKER.html`
- [ ] Set a 90-day reminder: "Re-engage {NAME} — {Project Name}"
- [ ] Add to quarterly outreach list (send 1 value-add email per quarter)

## The Follow-Up Rules

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| **Always add value** | Don't just "check in." Give them a reason to read your email. |
| **Multi-channel** | Email + LinkedIn + (optional SMS). Different channels catch different attention. |
| **Space them out** | Day 0, 2, 5, 9, 12, 14. Early touches build relationship. Later touches trigger action. |
| **Never apologize for following up** | "Sorry to bother you" weakens your position. You're doing them a service by staying organized. |
| **Close the loop** | Day 14 close-out protects your mental energy. Open loops drain focus. |
| **Personalize every touch** | No templates feel like templates. Reference 1 specific detail from your call. |

## Tracking in LEAD_TRACKER.html

## What If They Reply "Still Thinking"?

> "Totally understand — smart to think it through. What's the one thing you're still unsure about? If I can clear that up, great. If not, no rush."

**Then restart the cadence from Day 5** (skip Days 0-2 since they've already seen those).

# Client Testimonial System — AusAI Tech

> **Purpose:** Systematized, multi-channel testimonial gathering. Turn scattered asks into a repeatable system that produces video, written, LinkedIn, and case-study testimonials for every client.
> **When to use:** Throughout the client lifecycle — not just at offboarding. Multiple low-friction asks > one big ask at the end.
> **Prerequisite:** `CLIENT_OFFBOARDING.md` (3-Ask System), `First_Client_Onboarding.md` (kickoff DM), `CASE_STUDY_TEMPLATE.md`

---

## The Testimonial Philosophy

The worst way to get testimonials: one big ask at the end of a project.
The best way: multiple small, low-friction asks throughout the engagement.

Most consultants ask once. You'll ask 5 times. Not because you're pushy — because you make it easy.

---

## The 5-Ask System (When + What + How)

| # | When | What You're Asking | Format | Friction Level |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Kickoff call | Permission to document the project | "Is it OK if I use an anonymised version as a portfolio piece?" | Zero (just verbal yes) |
| **2** | Mid-project praise | Capture spontaneous positive feedback | "That's great to hear — mind if I jot that down?" | Zero (they just said it) |
| **3** | Delivery handoff | Written testimonial | "A quick sentence I can use on my site?" | Low (soft ask from offboarding) |
| **4** | 14-day check-in | Short video or LinkedIn recommendation | "If you've got 2 minutes, a quick Loom or LinkedIn rec goes a long way." | Medium (but warm relationship) |
| **5** | 30-day close-out | Final ask + case study permission | "Still open to that testimonial if the system's working well." | Low (final gentle nudge) |

**Goal:** By Ask 5, at least 60% of clients will have given you SOMETHING. Even if only 1 of 5 converts, that's a testimonial.

---

## Ask 1: Kickoff Permission (Minute 2 of Kickoff Call)

**Script:**

> "One quick thing before we dive in — once we build this, I'd love to use an anonymised version as a portfolio piece. No company name, no proprietary data, nothing identifying. Just 'built an automation that saved a client 12 hours a week' type thing. Cool?"

**Why this works:**
- Asked BEFORE value is delivered (they're optimistic, not critical)
- \"Anonymised\" removes the privacy objection
- \"Cool?\" is the lowest-friction close possible

**If they hesitate:** "No pressure at all — we can revisit once you've seen the results." (Don't push.)

**Record it:** Note in `LEAD_TRACKER.html` notes: "Portfolio permission: Yes / Pending."

---

## Ask 2: Mid-Project Praise Capture

The most organic testimonial source — they're happy, they say something nice, you capture it.

**Recognition triggers (listen for these):**

| They Say... | You Say... |
|---|---|
| "Wow, that was fast" | "Thanks! Mind if I note that down for a testimonial?" |
| "This is exactly what we needed" | "That's fantastic to hear. OK if I quote you on that?" |
| "Our team loves the new system" | "Made my day. Cool if I use that feedback?" |
| "You saved us so much time" | "That's the goal. Mind if I save that for the case study?" |
| "Why didn't we do this years ago?" | (Smile.) "Best feedback I could ask for. Can I quote you?" |

**The golden rule:** If they spontaneously praise, ask to capture it within 5 seconds. The window closes fast.

**Record it:** Copy their exact words into a "testimonials.md" draft file or the client notes in `LEAD_TRACKER.html`.

## Ask 3: Written Testimonial (Handoff Call)

From `CLIENT_OFFBOARDING.md` Phase 3, Ask 1 — but with more specificity:

**Script:**

> "If you've got 60 seconds, I'd love a quick sentence for my site. Three prompts to make it easy: 1) What was the problem before? 2) What did we build? 3) What's different now? Even one sentence on any of those is gold."

**Follow-up email template (send within 1 hour of handoff):**

> **Subject:** Quick testimonial request — 60 seconds
>
> Hi {NAME},
>
> Hope the new system is running smoothly. Quick favour: if you've got 60 seconds, a short testimonial I can use on my site. Any of these prompts work:
>
> 1. What was the problem before we worked together?
> 2. What did Karma build / deliver?
> 3. What's different now (time saved, cost reduced, stress eliminated)?
>
> Even one sentence is massive for me. Reply to this email or drop it in Slack. Real > polished — your actual words are perfect.
>
> If you'd rather not, no worries at all — just ignore this.
>
> Best,
> Karma

## Ask 4: Video or LinkedIn Recommendation (Day 14 Check-In)

Higher-friction ask, but much higher impact. A video testimonial is 10× more persuasive than text.

**Option A — LinkedIn Recommendation (lowest friction of the two):**

Send them your LinkedIn profile link and say:

> "If you've got 2 minutes, a quick LinkedIn recommendation about our project would mean the world. No need to overthink it — 2-3 sentences about what we built and how it's working. Here's my profile: [LinkedIn URL]"

**Option B — Short Video (Loom/phone):**

> "One more thing — and this is a bigger ask so feel free to ignore. I'm collecting 30-60 second video testimonials from clients. Just you on camera saying what the system does for you. I can send a couple of prompt questions if that helps. No pressure at all if video isn't your thing."

**Prompt questions for video (send these):**
1. "What was the biggest frustration before the automation?"
2. "What does the system do for you now?"
3. "Would you recommend working with Karma? Why?"

## Ask 5: Final Gentle Nudge (Day 30 Check-In)

From `CLIENT_OFFBOARDING.md` Phase 6, the 30-day close-out email. Add this line:

> "If you haven't had a chance yet, I'd still love a brief testimonial — even one sentence helps enormously: just reply to this email."

## Multi-Format Testimonial Assets

Once you have raw testimonials, reformat them for every channel:

| Channel | Format | How to Create |
|---|---|---|
| **Website** | Text quote + name + company | Paste into your portfolio page with their permission |
| **LinkedIn** | LinkedIn recommendation (native) | Ask them to write directly on your profile |
| **Fiverr** | Fiverr review (native) | Ask past Fiverr clients to leave reviews |
| **Proposal docs** | Quote in sidebar of `Proposal_Template.html` | Add a testimonials section |
| **Discovery call** | Verbal reference | "My last client in {industry} said: '{quote}'" |
| **Case study page** | Full before/after with testimonial | Use `CASE_STUDY_TEMPLATE.md` |
| **Social proof post** | LinkedIn/Twitter post with quote | "Client feedback that made my week:" + quote |
| **Email signature** | Rotating quote in `EMAIL_SIGNATURE.html` | "Recent client: '{quote}'" |

## Testimonial Quality Tiers

Not all testimonials are equal. Prioritise these qualities:

| Quality | Example (Weak) | Example (Strong) |
|---|---|---|
| **Specific numbers** | "Karma was great." | "Karma's automation saved us 12 hours/week and cut response time from 4 hours to 45 seconds." |
| **Named problem** | "He helped with automation." | "Our support tickets were burying us. Karma built an AI pipeline that auto-resolves 78% of them." |
| **Real outcome** | "Good experience." | "We redeployed 2 staff to revenue-generating work. The build paid for itself in 3 weeks." |
| **Personal touch** | "Professional service." | "Karma was direct, no fluff, and actually delivered what he said he would — on time." |

**When you get a weak testimonial:** Reply with "This is perfect — would you mind if I add one specific number? Like 'saved us about X hours'?" Most people are happy to add one detail.

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Ask 1 permission rate | 90%+ | They haven't seen results yet — this is easy |
| Ask 2 capture rate | 50%+ | You'll miss some spontaneous praise |
| Ask 3 written testimonial | 50%+ | Half of clients will write something |
| Ask 4 LinkedIn/video | 20-30% | Higher friction, lower response |
| Ask 5 final nudge | 10-20% | Last chance — some late responders |
| **Overall: at least 1 testimonial per client** | **60-70%** | Systems work |

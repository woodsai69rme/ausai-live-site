# 🚪 EXIT STRATEGY — AusAI Tech

**Purpose:** A clear, pre-planned decision framework for what to do if the A$5k-in-30-days goal isn't met.  
**When to open this:** Day 30 if revenue <A$3,000. Or any day you're considering quitting.

> **Why plan for failure?** Because panic decisions are bad decisions. Having an exit strategy removes fear — you know exactly what you'll do if things don't work. Paradoxically, this makes you more likely to succeed (no fear-based decisions).

## 1. THE DECISION TREE

```
At Day 30: Revenue = $X

    X ≥ $5,000?
    ├── YES → 🟢 Keep going. You're on track. Revisit at Day 60.
    └── NO  → X ≥ $3,000?
              ├── YES → 🟡 PIVOT. Something's working but not fast enough.
              │         → Go to Path A.
              └── NO  → X ≥ $1,000?
                        ├── YES → 🟠 SIDE HUSTLE. Revenue exists but isn't
                        │         sustainable yet. Go to Path B.
                        └── NO  → Have you had 5+ discovery calls?
                                  ├── YES → Pipeline exists but not closing.
                                  │         → Investigate close rate. Fix or call it.
                                  └── NO  → 🔴 No traction.
                                            → Go to Path C (or skip to §8 "Try Again")
```

## 2. TRIAGING YOUR RESULTS

Before choosing a path, diagnose WHY the target wasn't hit.

### Diagnostic Questions

| Symptom | Possible Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| 0 discovery calls booked | Outreach isn't working | Fix targeting, messaging, or channel |
| 5+ calls, 0 proposals | Call script isn't converting | Review MEDDIC qualifiers, objection handling |
| 5+ proposals, 0 closes | Pricing, trust, or timing issue | Check proposal follow-up, add demos, lower risk (money-back) |
| 3+ closes but low revenue | Pricing too low | Raise prices. See `PRICING_PSYCHOLOGY_GUIDE.md` |
| Clients love the work but slow to pay | Cash flow, not value, is the issue | Require upfront payment. Offer payment plans. |
| Everything works but burned out | Pacing issue | Reduce outreach to 3 days/week. Delegate admin. |

## 3. PATH A: PIVOT (KEEP GOING)

**Use when:** Revenue A$3,000–A$4,999. Or strong pipeline (3+ proposals out) but delayed closes.

### Pivot Options

| Pivot | What Changes | Effort | Expected Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Narrow your niche** | Instead of "any business" → "Australian e-commerce brands" or "accounting firms" | 1 day to rewrite outreach | Higher response rate, premium pricing |
| **Flip from outbound to inbound** | Double down on YouTube Shorts + SEO instead of cold DMs | Shift time, not add time | Slower start, compounds month 2-3 |
| **Raise prices 2×** | A$500 → A$1,000 minimum engagement | Just change the numbers | Fewer clients, same revenue, less work |
| **Bundle a product with service** | Sell the n8n Template Pack (A$67) + implementation (A$997) as a bundle (A$897) | 2 hrs to create bundle page | Higher conversion, product covers marketing costs |
| **Add a partner/reseller** | Find a web agency, accountant, or MSP who can resell your audits | 5-10 hrs outreach to partners | Pipeline from referrals without cold outreach |

### Pivot Timeline

- **Day 30–37:** Implement the pivot (1 week)
- **Day 37–60:** Measure new results
- **Day 60:** If A$3,000+ this month, pivot is working → keep going. If <A$2,000, move to Path B or C.

## 4. PATH B: PARALLEL TRACK (SIDE HUSTLE)

**Use when:** Revenue A$1,000–A$2,999. Consulting has promise but isn't full-time income yet.

### The 50/50 Split

| Time Block | Consulting | Backup Income |
|---|---|---|
| Mornings (4 hrs) | Outreach + client work | — |
| Afternoons (4 hrs) | — | Upwork/Fiverr gig work, contract dev work, or part-time job |

### Backup Income Options (Ranked by AusAI Skill Transfer)

| Option | Monthly Income | Skill Fit | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Upwork automation gigs** | A$1,000–A$3,000 | 100% | 15-20 hrs/week |
| **Contract Python/JS dev** | A$3,000–A$6,000 | 70% | 20-30 hrs/week |
| **n8n/Zapier template sales** (Gumroad) | A$200–A$800 | 100% | 5 hrs/week (passive after build) |
| **Part-time IT support/helpdesk** | A$1,500–A$2,500 | 30% | 15-20 hrs/week |
| **Retail/hospitality** | A$1,200–A$2,000 | 0% | 15-20 hrs/week |

### Parallel Track Rules

- **Consulting gets mornings** (your best energy goes to YOUR business)
- **Re-evaluate monthly:** When consulting hits A$3,000+/mo for 2 consecutive months, drop the backup.
- **Don't let backup income become the plan.** It's a bridge, not a destination.

## 5. PATH C: GRACEFUL CLOSE

**Use when:** Revenue <A$1,000 AND no pipeline. Or you've decided to move on.

### Closing Steps (Graceful, Professional)

1. **Notify active clients** (if any): "I'm winding down my consulting practice to pursue another opportunity. I'll complete our current engagement by {date} and provide full documentation for handoff."
2. **Refer clients out:** If you know another automation consultant, offer a warm intro. (Professional courtesy — they may return the favour.)
3. **Deliver all assets:** Source code, login credentials, documentation. Don't hold anything hostage.
4. **Send final invoices:** Collect outstanding payments.
5. **Archive everything:** Keep the portfolio for future use. Close subscriptions (Zapier, Make, n8n hosting, domain).
6. **Update LinkedIn:** "After building AI automations for {N} businesses, I'm taking a break from consulting. Open to interesting opportunities in {area}."

### Financial Close-Out

- **Cancel subscriptions** immediately (saves A$50–A$150/mo)
- **Let domain expire** or sell it
- **Keep Gumroad products live** (passive income — zero ongoing cost)
- **Keep LinkedIn/portfolio** (for your next chapter)

## 6. FINANCIAL RUN-OUT CALCULATOR

How long can you keep going before you MUST have income?

```
Monthly burn rate:   A$______ (rent, food, bills, subscriptions)
Current savings:     A$______ 
Monthly consulting:  A$______ (current run rate)

Runway (months) = savings / (burn rate - consulting income)

Example:
  Burn: A$2,500/mo
  Savings: A$5,000
  Consulting: A$1,000/mo
  Runway = 5,000 / (2,500 - 1,000) = 3.3 months

If runway < 2 months: Start Path B NOW.
If runway < 4 months: Have Path B lined up.
If runway > 6 months: You have time to pivot.
```

### Warning Triggers

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Runway < 3 months AND revenue <A$2,000/mo | Start Path B immediately |
| Savings < A$2,000 | Stop all non-essential spending. Apply for backup income. |
| No revenue for 4+ weeks | Reassess. This isn't working. Pivot hard or close. |

## 8. THE "TRY AGAIN" OPTION

**If you close:** You can always restart. The assets are preserved.

- **Domain:** Let it expire or park it. It's A$10-15/yr to keep.
- **Gumroad products:** They keep selling with zero effort.
- **GitHub Pages site:** Stays live at zero cost. Update the landing page with "On hiatus — back in 2026."
- **Portfolio:** The case studies don't expire. They get better with age.
- **Skills:** You'll only get better at automation over time.

**Restart trigger:** When you have 6+ months of runway saved, or a part-time job that covers expenses while consulting.

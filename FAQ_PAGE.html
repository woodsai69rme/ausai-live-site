AusAI Tech builds production-ready AI and automation systems for small-to-medium businesses. Common projects include: custom AI chatbots, n8n workflow automation, security audits of AI infrastructure, and local AI media studios (ComfyUI + Ollama). Everything is built to run — not just prototype — with clear documentation and handoff.

Timelines are fixed in the Statement of Work before work begins. If delays occur, you'll know immediately — not on the original deadline day.
Is my data safe?
Yes. Three principles guide every build:

1. Local-first where possible: Sensitive AI workloads (LLMs, image generation) can run entirely on your own hardware (Ollama, ComfyUI) — no data ever leaves your network.
2. No model training on your data: I do not use client data to train or fine-tune models. Your inputs stay yours.
3. Clear data handling: Every SOW specifies who owns what data, how long it's retained, and how it's deleted post-delivery. If you need an NDA, I'm happy to sign one before the audit.
What AI models and tools do you use?
I match the tool to the job — no vendor lock-in. Common stack:

• LLMs: OpenRouter (routes 50+ providers), Ollama (local), OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepSeek
• Automation: n8n (self-hosted), Zapier (if already in use)
• Vector / RAG: PostgreSQL + pgvector, Supabase, Pinecone
• Image / Video: ComfyUI, Stable Diffusion, Juggernaut-XL
• Hosting: GitHub Pages, Netlify, self-hosted VPS

If you already have tools in place, I integrate with them rather than replace.
Do you work with our existing systems?
Absolutely. Most clients already have CRMs, email platforms, or cloud setups. I build around what you have — adding AI and automation layers rather than forcing a rip-and-replace. During the free audit, I'll map your current stack and identify the highest-ROI integration points.
What if I'm not satisfied with the result?
Every project includes a 14-day warranty post-delivery. If something doesn't work as specified in the SOW, I'll fix it within 48 hours at no charge.

The SOW also defines a single, measurable acceptance criterion — so "done" is never ambiguous. If we genuinely can't meet that criterion, we'll discuss a proportional refund or revised scope. Fairness is non-negotiable.

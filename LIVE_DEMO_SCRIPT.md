# Live Demo Script — AusAI Tech (Discovery Call Companion)

> **Purpose:** A 5-minute live demo script to use during discovery calls. Shows real automation value in real time. Boosts close rate from 22% to 38%+ per research.
> **When to use:** At minute 7-12 of the discovery call (Phase 3: Value), after the prospect has described their pain.
> **Prerequisite:** `Discovery_Call_Script.md`, `BEFORE_AFTER_DEMO.html` (open in browser), `AUTOMATION_ROI_CALCULATOR.html`

---

## The Demo Principle

**Show, don't tell.** Every prospect has been pitched by 10 people this month. Nobody has shown them something working in real time. A 5-minute live demo makes you unforgettable.

**What you need open before the call:**
- `BEFORE_AFTER_DEMO.html` in a browser tab (screen-share ready)
- `AUTOMATION_ROI_CALCULATOR.html` in a second tab (for the numbers close)
- A real n8n workflow OR a screenshot of one (proof you actually build things)

---

## The 5-Minute Demo Structure

| Time | What You Do | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00–0:30 | Transition from pain to demo | Bridge moment |
| 0:30–2:00 | Screen-share BEFORE_AFTER_DEMO.html | Visual proof |
| 2:00–3:30 | Map THEIR pain to the demo | Personalisation |
| 3:30–4:30 | Show ROI calculation live | Numbers close |
| 4:30–5:00 | Pivot to next step | Call to action |

---

## Minute 0:00–0:30 — The Transition

**Script (bridge from Phase 2 pain discovery):**

> "So what you're describing — {their pain} eating {X hours / Y dollars} — this is exactly what I solve. Let me show you something real quick. I'm going to share my screen."

*Share screen. Open BEFORE_AFTER_DEMO.html.*

> "This is a customer support workflow a client came to me with. Before they worked with me — 4-hour response time, 2 people full-time, $78k a year in labour. After — 45 seconds, mostly automated, $3k a year. This isn't a mockup. This is the actual system."

---

## Minute 0:30–2:00 — Walk Through the Demo

**Don't just show it. Walk them through the story:**

> "Here's what changed: before — every email sat 4 hours before someone even read it. After — AI classifies it instantly and auto-resolves the simple stuff. 78% never touches a human. The 22% that does gets routed to the right person with a draft reply pre-written. Human just reviews and sends."

**Point at specific stats:**
- "4.2 hours → 45 seconds. That's a 99.7% reduction."
- "2 people full-time → 0.2 FTE. They redeployed those people to do actual client work."
- "A$78k/year → A$3,120/year. That's a 96% cost reduction."

**Then pause.** Let them absorb. Don't fill the silence.

---

## Minute 2:00–3:30 — Map to THEIR Situation

**Script:**

> "Now, your situation is {their described pain}. It's similar in principle — the key difference is {their unique factor}. Here's how I'd adapt this for you..."

**Customize on the fly (practice these pivots):**

| If They Said... | Customize To... |
|---|---|
| "Customer emails are burying us" | "Instead of support tickets, we'd classify inbound sales inquiries. Same AI pipeline, different categories." |
| "We spend hours on data entry" | "Instead of email parsing, we'd connect to your {CRM/spreadsheet} API. Webhook fires → AI structures data → auto-fills." |
| "Our team does repetitive reporting" | "n8n pulls from {data source} every Monday, AI writes the narrative summary, auto-emailed to stakeholders." |
| "Leads fall through the cracks" | "Every form submission → AI scores lead quality → high-value leads get Slack alert + auto-drafted reply. Low-value get nurture sequence." |

**Key:** Always use THEIR words. "You said 'burying us' — that's exactly what the before state looked like for my other client."

---

## Minute 3:30–4:30 — The Numbers Close

**Switch to AUTOMATION_ROI_CALCULATOR.html tab:**

> "Let's punch in YOUR numbers quick. You said {X hours/week} on {task}, done by {Y people}, at roughly ${Z}/hour."

*Fill the sliders with their numbers. Read the result aloud:*

> "Okay — that's A${weekWaste} per week, A${yearWaste} per year being spent on this manually. Automating roughly 80% of that saves A${savable}/year. Our A$1,497 build pays for itself in {payback} months."

**Then pause.** Let them see the number. This is the most powerful moment in the call.

---

## Minute 4:30–5:00 — The Pivot to Close

**Script:**

> "So the question isn't really whether this is worth doing — the math is clear. The question is: do you want to start with the A$497 audit that maps exactly how we'd build this for you, or jump straight to the A$1,497 build and have it running in 14 days?"

**This is the Assumptive Close.** You're not asking IF they want to work with you. You're asking WHICH WAY.

## Demo Do's and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| ✅ Screen-share the BEFORE_AFTER_DEMO.html | ❌ Share your desktop with 37 open tabs |
| ✅ Use THEIR numbers in the ROI calculator | ❌ Use "typical" numbers — always customise |
| ✅ Pause after showing savings — let it sink in | ❌ Rush through the numbers |
| ✅ Have both demo pages pre-loaded before the call | ❌ Fumble with URLs while they wait |
| ✅ Practice this script 3x before your first real call | ❌ Wing it — you'll stumble |
| ✅ Mention this was a "real client" (even if portfolio piece) | ❌ Overhype or fabricate specific client names |

---

## If the Demo Fails (Technical Backup)

Technology fails. Have a backup:

> "Looks like my screen share is being finicky. No worries — I'll send you the before/after demo right now. Check your inbox."

**Pre-send backup:** Have a draft email ready with either: a) the link to the deployed `BEFORE_AFTER_DEMO.html` page, or b) a screenshot/PDF export of the demo page (if not deployed yet). Subject: "The automation demo I mentioned."

**Then pivot to verbal:**

> "While that lands, let me describe what happens: customer emails come in, AI classifies them, 78% auto-resolve, the other 22% get a pre-written draft. Response time drops from hours to seconds."

## Practice Routine

Before your first real demo call:

1. **Dry run 1:** Read this script aloud. Time yourself. Should be ~5 min.
2. **Dry run 2:** Record yourself (Loom or phone). Watch it back. Cut filler words.
3. **Dry run 3:** Do it with a friend on Zoom. Screen-share. Get feedback on pacing.
4. **Real call 1:** Accept it won't be perfect. Debrief after — what worked, what didn't.
5. **Real call 3+:** You'll stop reading the script and it'll feel natural.

# Quarterly Business Review Template — AusAI Tech

> **Purpose:** Structured 30-min quarterly review for retainer clients. Demonstrates ROI, surfaces upsell opportunities, and prevents churn by showing continuous value.
> **When to use:** Every 90 days for every AI Partner (A$2,497/mo) retainer client.
> **Prerequisite:** `RETAINER_AGREEMENT.html`, `UPSELL_PLAYBOOK.md`, `Revenue_Dashboard_Static.html`

---

## The QBR Philosophy

Retainer clients don't churn because the automation stops working. They churn because they forget what you're doing for them. A QBR reminds them — with numbers, not promises.

**Goal of every QBR:**
1. Prove ROI (with their numbers)
2. Preview what's next (roadmap)
3. Surface one upsell or expansion opportunity
4. Renew commitment (verbal or written)

---

## QBR Agenda (30 Minutes)

| Time | Section | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00–3:00 | Warm open + agenda | Set frame: "This is about making sure you're getting maximum value." |
| 3:00–10:00 | **ROI Review** — what we built, what it saved | Prove value with THEIR numbers |
| 10:00–18:00 | **System Health** — uptime, issues resolved, SLA performance | Show operational excellence |
| 18:00–25:00 | **Roadmap** — next quarter's priorities | Preview value, surface upsell |
| 25:00–30:00 | **Open discussion** — concerns, changes, new needs | Listen, retain, upsell |

## Section 1: ROI Review (3:00–10:00)

### Slide/Visual: The Numbers That Matter

| Labour cost saved | A${X} | A${Y} | A${Z} |
| Response time (if applicable) | {X} sec | — | — |
| Auto-resolution rate | {X}% | — | — |
| Tickets/issues auto-handled | {X} | {Y} | {Z} |
| System uptime | {X}% | — | — |

### Script

> "Let's start with the numbers. This quarter, {system name} handled {X} {tickets/workflows/tasks}, saving roughly {Y} hours — that's about A${Z} in labour costs. Since we started, you're up to {total} hours saved. That means the system has paid for itself {X} times over."

**If the numbers are good:** "You're getting {ROI multiple}× return on your retainer investment."

**If the numbers are modest:** "The system is still ramping. As we add {next quarter's priority}, these numbers should {2-3×}."

## Section 3: Roadmap Preview (18:00–25:00)

### Slide: What's Next

```
NEXT QUARTER PRIORITIES:
1. {Priority 1} — Impact: {X hrs saved / Y% improvement}
   Effort: {X} hours | Included in retainer ✅
2. {Priority 2} — Impact: {X}
   Effort: {X} hours | Included ✅
3. {Priority 3} — Impact: {X}
   Effort: {X} hours | Requires scope expansion (+A${X}/month or one-off A${Y})
```

### Script

> "Looking ahead: priorities 1 and 2 are covered by your current retainer. Priority 3 — {describe briefly} — would require expanding scope slightly. Want me to include that in the plan, or keep it on the backlog?"

**This is your upsell moment.** Priority 3 should be something genuinely valuable that isn't covered by the current retainer. Frame it as "here's what's possible" not "buy more from me."

---

## Section 4: Open Discussion (25:00–30:00)

### Questions to Ask

- "Anything changed in your business that the automation should adapt to?"
- "Any workflows you wish were automated that aren't yet?"
- "How's the team finding the system? Any friction points?"
- "Any upcoming changes (new tools, team changes, compliance requirements) I should know about?"

### Close

> "Great — I'll send the QBR summary and next quarter's plan by end of day. Same time in 90 days?"

## When a Client Is At-Risk (Red QBR)

If the numbers are weak, the client is unhappy, or you sense churn risk — take a different approach:

1. **Lead with listening:** "Before I share numbers, how are YOU feeling about the system?"
2. **Acknowledge gaps:** If something isn't working, own it: "I've noticed {X} isn't where we want it. Here's my plan to fix it."
3. **Propose a reset:** "I'd suggest we focus next quarter entirely on {their top pain point}. No new features — just making the existing system bulletproof."
4. **Offer a concession if needed:** One month at reduced scope while you fix issues. Better to lose A$2,497 for a month than lose the entire retainer.

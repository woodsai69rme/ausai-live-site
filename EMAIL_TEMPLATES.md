> **Purpose:** Copy/paste-ready emails for every client touchpoint. No thinking required — just fill `{{placeholders}}` and send.
> **Tone:** Professional, concise, outcome-focused. Never apologetic about pricing. Always offer a clear next step.

AusAI Tech

- "Reply" CTA — low friction next step

## 2. Cold Lead Nurture Sequence (5 emails over 14 days)

> **Trigger:** Prospect replied to LinkedIn DM or filled a form but hasn't booked a call yet.
> **Goal:** Move from "interested" to "booked" without being pushy.
> **Cadence:** Day 0 (immediately), Day 2, Day 5, Day 9, Day 14.

---

### Email 1 — Day 0 (immediately after they reply/express interest)

**Subject:** Quick question, {{first_name}}

```
Hi {{first_name}},

Thanks for the reply — {{reference something they said}}.

I help {{target_audience}} fix {{pain_point}} using AI automation. Typical result: {{specific_outcome}} in under {{timeframe}}.

I do a free 30-min AI Audit where I look at your current setup and tell you exactly what's costing you money + what I'd fix first. No pitch — just a diagnostic.

Worth a conversation? Book here: {{calendly_url}}

— karma
```

---

### Email 2 — Day 2 (value-add, no pitch)

**Subject:** The #1 mistake I see with {{relevant_topic}}

```
Hi {{first_name}},

Quick value-drop — no pitch.

The #1 mistake I see {{target_audience}} make with AI/automation:

They buy tools before they map workflows.

Result: 3-5 disconnected apps, duplicate data, staff ignoring half of them.

The fix (takes 20 min):
1. Write down the ONE task that eats most of your time
2. Map who does what, in what order, with what data
3. Only THEN look for a tool that fits that flow

Most people skip step 2. Industry data suggests roughly half of AI pilot projects stall before production — often because workflow mapping is skipped [source: McKinsey / Gartner AI adoption surveys; treat as directional, not precise].

If you want me to run this 20-min exercise on your actual workflow, the audit link is still open: {{calendly_url}}

— karma
```

## 5. Delay Notification Email (send as soon as delay is known)

**Subject:** {{Project Name}} — Timeline update ({{new_delivery_date}})

```
Hi {{first_name}},

Quick heads-up on {{Project Name}}.

Original delivery: {{original_date}}
New delivery: {{new_date}}
Reason: {{honest_reason}}

WHAT THIS MEANS:
  • The scope hasn't changed — same deliverables
  • {{specific_impact_or_none}}
  • I'm prioritizing {{specific_focus}} to make up time

WHAT I'M DOING ABOUT IT:
  • {{action_1}}
  • {{action_2}}

COMPENSATION:
  {{if_delay_is_your_fault}} As this delay is on my side, I'm applying a A${{credit_amount}} credit to your final invoice.
  {{if_delay_is_client}} Once {{client_blocker}} is resolved, I'll resume immediately. Current ETA holds.
  {{if_external}} This is due to {{external_factor}} outside both our control. I'm monitoring daily and will update you {{frequency}}.

If this new timeline doesn't work for you, reply and we'll find a solution.

— karma
```

**Rules for delay emails:**
- Send ASAP — never wait until the original deadline
- Own it if it's your fault — credits build trust
- Never blame the client even if it IS their fault — just state facts neutrally
- Always offer a path forward

## 🎯 When to Send What

```
PROSPECT JOURNEY:
  Interested (replied to DM) ──► Email 1 (Day 0)
  No booking after 2 days ────► Email 2 (Day 2) — value-add
  No booking after 5 days ────► Email 3 (Day 5) — social proof
  No booking after 9 days ────► Email 4 (Day 9) — scarcity
  No booking after 14 days ───► Email 5 (Day 14) — breakup

CLIENT JOURNEY:
  Call ends ──────────────────► Post-discovery follow-up (1 hour)
  Deposit clears ─────────────► Kickoff email (immediately)
  Mid-project ────────────────► Progress update (~Day 10)
  Delay known ────────────────► Delay notification (ASAP)
  Delivery complete ──────────► Final delivery + testimonial (Day 32)
```

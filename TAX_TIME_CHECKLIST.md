# Tax Time Checklist — AusAI Tech (EOFY Prep for Sole Traders)

> **Purpose:** Complete EOFY tax preparation in one session. What to gather, how to organise, what to send your accountant.
> **When to use:** June (EOFY is June 30 in Australia). Start 2 weeks before you meet your accountant.
> **Prerequisite:** `COMPLIANCE_CHECKLIST.md` (ongoing compliance), `Revenue_Dashboard_Static.html` (revenue records)

---

## Tax Time Quick Facts (Australia, Sole Trader)

| Fact | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Financial year** | July 1 – June 30 |
| **Tax return deadline** | October 31 (self-lodged) or May 15 (if using a registered tax agent) |
| **BAS lodgement** (if GST registered) | Quarterly: July 28, Oct 28, Feb 28, Apr 28 |
| **PAYG instalments** | ATO will notify you if required (usually once you earn >$18,200) |
| **Record keeping** | 5 years from lodgement date |

### 2025-26 Individual Tax Rates (Australian Resident)

| Taxable Income | Tax on This Income |
|---|---|
| $0 – $18,200 | Nil |
| $18,201 – $45,000 | 16c for each $1 over $18,200 |
| $45,001 – $135,000 | $4,288 + 30c for each $1 over $45,000 |
| $135,001 – $190,000 | $31,288 + 37c for each $1 over $135,000 |
| $190,001+ | $51,638 + 45c for each $1 over $190,000 |

*Medicare Levy: additional 2% of taxable income (not included above).*

## Phase 4: BAS Lodgement (If GST Registered)

If you're NOT GST registered (revenue < $75k/year): **Skip this section.**

If you ARE GST registered:

- [ ] **BAS lodged for Q1 (Jul-Sep):** Due Oct 28
- [ ] **BAS lodged for Q2 (Oct-Dec):** Due Feb 28
- [ ] **BAS lodged for Q3 (Jan-Mar):** Due Apr 28
- [ ] **BAS lodged for Q4 (Apr-Jun):** Due Jul 28

### BAS Numbers to Report

| Item | Where to Find It |
|---|---|
| **G1** — Total sales | Sum of all invoices (including GST) |
| **1A** — GST on sales | G1 ÷ 11 (10% GST) |
| **G11** — Total purchases (with GST) | Sum of all business expenses with GST |
| **1B** — GST on purchases | G11 ÷ 11 |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Not claiming home office expenses | You CAN claim a portion of rent, electricity, internet, phone. Use the ATO's fixed rate (67c per hour worked from home) or actual cost method. |
| Mixing personal and business expenses | Separate them now — your accountant will charge you to untangle them |
| Forgetting to claim super contributions | Personal super contributions ARE tax-deductible. Claim them. |
| Missing the Fiverr 20% fee deduction | Fiverr takes 20% of every gig. That's a business expense — deduct it. |
| Not tracking vehicle use for business | Keep a logbook or use the cents-per-km method (up to 5,000 km). |
| Claiming 100% of phone/internet for business | Be realistic. Estimate business use % honestly. |
| Throwing away receipts under $75 | You can claim small expenses without a receipt, but having evidence is better. |
| Waiting until October 30 | Use a tax agent and get until May 15. Less stress, professional review. |

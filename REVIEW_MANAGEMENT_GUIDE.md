## 2. THE 3-ASK REVIEW SYSTEM

Don't ask for a review once. Ask 3 times across the client lifecycle.

### Ask 1: Mid-Project Praise (Week 2-3 of build)

```
"The Slack integration is looking great! If you're happy with how 
things are going, I'd love a quick Google/LinkedIn recommendation 
— it really helps other businesses find me.

No pressure if you'd rather wait until we're done.

Google review link: {your_google_review_link}
LinkedIn recommendation: {your_linkedin_url}"
```

**Why mid-project:** They're excited. The automation is taking shape. Don't wait until the end when the excitement fades.

### Ask 2: Post-Delivery (Day of handoff)

```
"Project delivered! 🎉 3 quick things as we wrap up:

1. Your documentation + walkthrough video are attached.
2. If you have 60 seconds, a review would mean the world:
   Google: {link}
   {Your platform}: {link}
3. I'll check in next week to make sure everything's running smoothly."
```

### Ask 3: The 14-Day Follow-Up (After warranty period)

```
"Hey {name} — hope the automation is saving you those {X} hours/week!

Quick favour: if you haven't had a chance to leave a review yet, 
here's the link one more time: {link}

This is the last time I'll ask — promise! 

And if there's anything not working right, please tell me that instead. 
I'd rather fix it than get a 5-star review on a broken system."
```

## 3. GOOGLE REVIEW GENERATION

### Setup (One-Time)

1. Create/claim your Google Business Profile (see `LOCAL_SEO_GUIDE.md`)
2. Generate your Google review link:
   - Go to Google Business Profile Manager
   - Click "Ask for reviews" → Copy the short link
   - It looks like: `https://g.page/r/XXXXX/review`
3. Save this link. Use it everywhere.

### Review Request Best Practices

- **Send the direct link** — don't make them search for your business
- **Text it** — SMS gets 3× higher response than email
- **Time it right:** Tuesday-Thursday, 10 AM or 2 PM
- **Give guidance:** "A sentence or two about your experience is perfect"
- **Never incentivise:** Google explicitly bans paid/fake reviews. Don't offer discounts for reviews.

### Where to Place Your Google Review Link

- Email signature
- Invoice footer
- Client portal (`CLIENT_PORTAL.html`)
- Post-project email
- WhatsApp/Slack message
- Business card (QR code)

## 4. FIVERR REVIEW VELOCITY

### Strategy

Fiverr's algorithm ranks gigs by review velocity — how fast you accumulate positive reviews after orders.

**Critical window:** First 48 hours after order completion. Ask within this window.

### Review Message (copy-paste)

```
Hi {buyer},

Your order is complete! If you're happy with the result, 
a quick review would really help my new gig get discovered:

{Fiverr will prompt you to leave a review}

If anything's not quite right, let me know before reviewing 
and I'll fix it immediately.

Thanks for trusting me with your automation!
```

### After 48 Hours (if no review)

One polite follow-up. Then stop.

## 5. UPWORK REVIEW STRATEGY

Same principles as Fiverr. Key differences:

- **JSS impact:** Reviews directly affect Job Success Score. Protect it.
- **Private feedback matters too:** Clients rate you 1-10 privately. This also affects JSS.
- **Refund before bad review:** If a client seems unhappy, offer a partial refund before they leave a review. A A$200 refund is cheaper than a 4.2 JSS.

## 7. HANDLING NEGATIVE REVIEWS

### The Golden Rules

1. **Respond within 24 hours** (publicly, professionally)
2. **Never argue.** "We disagreed about X" is fine. "The client is wrong" is not.
3. **Take it offline.** "I'd love to resolve this directly. Please email me at hello@ausai.tech."
4. **Don't ask them to delete it.** Unless it violates platform rules (fake, competitor, spam), it stays.

### Response Template

```
Hi {name},

Thank you for sharing your experience. I genuinely value your feedback.

I'm sorry to hear about {specific_issue}. This isn't the experience 
I aim to deliver, and I'd like to make it right.

I've sent you an email to discuss how I can resolve this. 
Please check your inbox (or spam folder).

{Your Name}
```

### When to Report a Review

- The review is from a competitor (provable)
- Contains harassment, hate speech, or personal attacks
- Contains false claims (e.g., "they never delivered" when you have proof)
- Is spam or unrelated to your service

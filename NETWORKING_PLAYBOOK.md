# Networking Playbook — AusAI Tech

> **Purpose:** Systematic in-person and virtual networking strategy for solo consultants. Where to go, what to say, how to follow up, how to convert contacts into clients.
> **When to use:** Ongoing. 1-2 events per month minimum.
> **Prerequisite:** `Discovery_Call_Script.md` (your pitch), `LINKEDIN_DM_TEMPLATES.md` (follow-up templates), `LEAD_TRACKER.html` (track contacts)

---

## The Networking Philosophy for Solo Consultants

You don't need to be at every event. You need to be at the RIGHT events — and you need a system that converts handshakes into discovery calls.

**The 1-1-1 rule per event:**
- 1 genuine connection (not 50 business cards)
- 1 follow-up sent within 24 hours
- 1 lead in your pipeline within 2 weeks (either direct or referral from that connection)

If an event doesn't produce at least 1 pipeline lead within a month, stop going.

## Event Types: Where to Go

### Tier 1: Highest-ROI (Go to These)

| Event Type | Examples | Why | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Industry meetups** | Sydney AI Meetup, Melbourne Tech Drinks, local SaaS founders groups | Your exact target market in one room | 1-2/month |
| **Co-working space events** | Fishburners, Hub Australia, WeWork community events | Casual, low-pressure, founder-heavy | 1/month |
| **Client industry events** (not tech) | E-commerce conferences, retail expos, healthcare summits | Where your CLIENTS hang out — not where tech people hang out | 1/quarter |

### Tier 2: Good ROI (Go When You Can)

| Event Type | Examples | Why | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Startup pitch nights** | Startmate, Techstars demo days | Founders actively spending money to grow | 1/month |
| **Hackathons (as mentor, not competitor)** | GovHack, Unearthed, university hackathons | Positions you as expert; mentees become clients | 1/quarter |
| **Online communities (active, not lurking)** | Indie Hackers, specific Slack/Discord communities | Lower effort, can do from home | Weekly |

### Tier 3: Low ROI (Skip Unless You Have a Specific Reason)

- Large conferences (SXSW, Web Summit) — too big, too noisy for solo consultant
- Generic "networking" events (Chamber of Commerce mixers) — rarely your ICP
- Webinars (as attendee) — passive, no real connection
- LinkedIn audio events — low conversion to actual relationships

## The 5-Minute Networking Conversation

You don't need 30 minutes. You need 5 focused minutes that end with a reason to follow up.

### Minute 0:00–1:00 — The Open

**Walk up to someone standing alone or in a group of 3.** The easiest opening:

> "Hey — I'm Karma. What brings you to this event?"

**Listen.** Don't talk about yourself yet. Let them tell you why they're there.

---

### Minute 1:00–2:00 — The Discovery

**Ask follow-up questions about what they said:**

> "Interesting — what's the biggest challenge in {their field} right now?"

> "How are you currently handling {their mentioned problem}?"

**Key:** You're doing a micro discovery call. Listen for pain that automation could solve.

---

### Minute 2:00–3:00 — Your Turn (One Sentence Only)

**When they ask what you do:**

> "I build AI automations for small businesses — the kind that save 10-20 hours a week on repetitive work. Customer support, data entry, reporting — that kind of thing."

**One sentence. Period.** If they want more, they'll ask. If they don't, you've planted the seed.

**If they ask for more:**

> "Most recently I built a customer support pipeline that auto-resolves 78% of emails. Client went from 2 full-time staff on tickets to 0.2 FTE. Response time dropped from 4 hours to 45 seconds."

Now you've given them a concrete result they can visualise.

---

### Minute 3:00–4:00 — The Handoff

**Don't ask "can I send you something?" (vague, low conversion). Ask:**

> "Would it be useful if I sent you that before/after demo? It's a 2-minute page showing the actual before and after stats from that project."

**If yes:** "What's the best email?" — take their card or type it into your phone.

**If they're a potential client:**

> "Sounds like the {pain they described} is eating some hours. Happy to do a quick 15-minute call this week — I'll tell you if automation makes sense for it, and if not, I'll give you a couple of free ideas you can implement yourself. Worth 15 minutes?"

---

### Minute 4:00–5:00 — The Exit

**Don't linger. End strong:**

> "Great meeting you — I'll send that demo over tonight. Enjoy the rest of the event."

**Then move on.** The person who leaves first is the one who looks busy and in demand.

## Post-Event System (Within 24 Hours)

### Step 1: Log in LEAD_TRACKER.html (5 min)

For every meaningful contact:
- Name, company, role
- What they're dealing with (the pain you identified)
- Source: "Networking — {Event Name} — {Date}"
- Next step: "Send demo" / "Book discovery call" / "Connect on LinkedIn"

### Step 2: LinkedIn Connection (Same Day)

Send a connection request with a note:

> "Great meeting you at {event} — enjoyed hearing about {their thing}. Would love to stay connected."

**Don't pitch in the LinkedIn request.** Just connect.

### Step 3: Follow-Up Email (Within 24 Hours)

> **Subject:** Great meeting you at {event}
>
> Hi {NAME},
>
> Enjoyed our chat about {their challenge} at {event} last night.
>
> As promised, here's the before/after automation demo I mentioned: [link to BEFORE_AFTER_DEMO.html]
>
> If you ever want to explore whether automation makes sense for {their use case}, happy to do a quick 15-minute call. No pitch — just a few ideas you can use either way.
>
> Best,
> Karma
> AusAI Tech

### Step 4: Calendar Reminder (Day 5)

If no response in 5 days, send one gentle follow-up:

> "Hi {NAME}, just circling back on my email from last week. No pressure — the demo link is there whenever you're curious. Cheers, Karma"

**If no response to follow-up:** Don't send a third. Move them to dormant in LEAD_TRACKER.html.

## Virtual Networking (Slack, Discord, Communities)

Same principles, adapted for text:

### The 3-Contribution Rule

Before you EVER pitch yourself in a community:

1. Answer 3 questions helpfully (demonstrate expertise)
2. Share 1 resource (article, tool, template)
3. THEN mention what you do (only if relevant)

**Example:**

> Question in #automation: "Anyone know how to handle rate limiting in n8n?"
>
> Your reply: "I hit this last month. Two approaches: 1) n8n's built-in retry on fail node with exponential backoff. 2) If you need more control, a code node with a simple while loop + sleep. Happy to share the snippet if useful."

This establishes expertise. The next time someone asks about automation, your name is associated with the answer.

## Conference Strategy (If You Go to One)

For larger events (500+ people):

| Before | During | After |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Research 5 target attendees on LinkedIn | ✅ Sit in the FRONT row of talks (speakers notice) | ✅ Post 1 LinkedIn takeaway within 24h |
| ✅ Pre-draft 3 connection request notes | ✅ Ask 1 question per talk (be remembered) | ✅ Tag speakers and new connections |
| ✅ Pack business cards (yes, still useful) | ✅ Skip the afterparties — go to the speaker dinner instead | ✅ Follow up with every meaningful contact |

**The conference secret:** The best networking happens at the SPEAKER DINNER, not the main event. If you can get into the speaker dinner (volunteer, know someone, buy a speaker ticket), your ROI 10×s.

## Hosting Your Own Event (Advanced)

Once you have 10+ local contacts, host a small event. This positions you as a community leader, not just an attendee.

**Event:** "AI for Small Business — 5 Automations You Can Build This Weekend"

- Venue: Co-working space (often free for members)
- Format: 20-min talk + 40-min Q&A + networking
- Cost: Free for attendees
- Follow-up: Every attendee gets the slides + your Calendly link

**This is the single highest-ROI networking activity.** You go from "person I met at an event" to "the person who taught me about AI automation."

## What NOT to Do

| Don't | Why | Do Instead |
|---|---|---|
| ❌ Pitch immediately after "what do you do?" | You haven't earned it | Listen first. Your pitch comes at minute 2-3 |
| ❌ Collect 20 business cards and email them all the same template | Generic = spam | Personalise every follow-up with 1 specific detail from your chat |
| ❌ Only attend tech events | You're talking to competitors, not clients | Go where your CLIENTS go — e-commerce events, retail expos |
| ❌ Talk to one person all night | 1 good contact ≠ 1 hour | 5-minute conversations, move on, follow up later |
| ❌ Drink at networking events | You're working, not socialising | One drink max. You're building a business, not a social life |

# 🤝 HIRING YOUR FIRST VA — AusAI Tech

**Purpose:** When and how to hire a Virtual Assistant to buy back your time from admin tasks.  
**Threshold:** Hire when you're spending 10+ hrs/week on non-billable admin.  
**Budget:** A$8–A$20/hr (Philippines-based, skilled VAs).

> **Why this matters:** Every hour you spend on admin is an hour you're NOT selling, building, or billing. A VA at A$10/hr frees you to bill A$85–A$150/hr. That's an 8-15× ROI.

## 1. WHEN TO HIRE

### Hire Immediately If

- You're spending 10+ hrs/week on admin (invoicing, scheduling, data entry, research)
- You've turned down a client because you were "too busy with admin"
- You're doing tasks someone at A$10/hr could do while you bill A$85/hr

### Wait If

- You have <3 active clients
- Revenue <A$3,000/mo (the A$400-800/mo VA cost is too high a percentage)
- You haven't systematised your processes yet (VA needs processes to follow)

### The Math

```
Without VA:
  30 hrs/week total
  15 hrs client work (billable) = A$1,275/week at A$85/hr
  15 hrs admin (unbillable) = A$0

With VA:
  30 hrs/week total
  25 hrs client work = A$2,125/week
   5 hrs managing VA = unbillable
  VA cost: 20 hrs × A$10/hr = A$200

Weekly gain: A$2,125 - A$200 = A$1,925 vs. A$1,275 before
Annual gain: ~A$33,800
```

## 2. WHAT A VA CAN DO

### ✅ Great for VA

| Task Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Admin** | Calendar management, email triage, file organisation |
| **Outreach support** | Research prospects, compile LinkedIn lists, draft initial messages |
| **Content repurposing** | Turn your blog/LinkedIn post into tweets, Reddit posts, email newsletter |
| **Data entry** | CRM updates, spreadsheet maintenance, invoice generation |
| **Research** | Competitor pricing, industry news, potential partner lists |
| **Customer support** | Answer FAQs, triage support requests, schedule calls |
| **Social media** | Schedule posts, respond to comments, track metrics |
| **Bookkeeping** | Categorise expenses, reconcile Stripe, prep for accountant |

### ❌ NOT for VA

| Task | Why Not | Do It Yourself |
|---|---|---|
| Client-facing calls | You're the brand. Clients buy YOU. | Discovery calls, QBRs |
| Technical build (automation) | Unless they're a specialist. General VAs can't do this. | Or hire a specialist/subcontractor |
| Strategic decisions | Pricing, positioning, which clients to take | You |
| Crisis communication | Needs your voice and judgment | You — use `CRISIS_COMMS_PLAYBOOK.md` |

## 3. WHERE TO FIND VAs

| Platform | Cost | Quality | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OnlineJobs.ph** | A$99/month (job listing) | High — dedicated Filipino VAs | Long-term, full-time or part-time |
| **Upwork** | Free to post, pay per hire | Medium-High | Project-based or short-term test |
| **Fiverr** | Free to browse | Medium | One-off tasks |
| **VA agencies** (e.g., VA Platinum, Virtual Assistant Philippines) | Higher hourly (A$15-25) | High — pre-vetted, managed | Hands-off, premium |

## 4. JOB POST TEMPLATE

```
Title: Virtual Assistant for AI Automation Consultancy (Part-Time)

I run a solo AI automation consultancy (AusAI Tech) helping businesses
automate repetitive work. I'm looking for a VA to handle admin, research,
and content support so I can focus on client work.

Hours: 10-20 hrs/week (flexible — can grow to full-time)
Rate: A$8-12/hr (depending on experience)
Timezone: Must overlap at least 4 hours with AEST (9 AM - 1 PM preferred)

Responsibilities:
1. Research prospective clients (LinkedIn, company websites)
2. Compile outreach lists with personalised notes
3. Repurpose my content across platforms (LinkedIn → Twitter → Reddit)
4. Manage my calendar + schedule calls
5. Generate draft invoices from templates
6. Track expenses + categorise for tax time
7. Monitor competitor pricing/activity (monthly)

Requirements:
- Excellent written English
- Experience with: Google Sheets, LinkedIn, Notion (or similar)
- Bonus: Familiarity with automation tools (Zapier, Make, n8n)
- Bonus: Experience supporting a freelancer/consultant before

To apply:
1. Send your resume + a 2-min Loom video introducing yourself
2. Include the phrase "automation rocks" in your subject line (filter test)
3. Share 1 example of how you organised something chaotic

I'll respond within 48 hours if there's a fit.
```

## 5. INTERVIEW & TEST TASK

### Interview Questions (30 min, Zoom)

1. "Tell me about a time you supported a busy person. How did you prioritise?"
2. "I'll give you 10 tasks. Which 3 do you do first and why?"
3. "How do you handle ambiguous instructions?"
4. "Describe a mistake you made at work and how you fixed it."
5. "What's your ideal working relationship with a manager?"

### Paid Test Task (2-3 hrs, A$30 flat)

Give all shortlisted candidates the SAME task:

```
Task: Research 20 Australian e-commerce businesses that might need automation.

Instructions:
1. Use LinkedIn to find AU-based e-commerce founders/ops managers
2. For each: name, title, company, company size, LinkedIn URL, 1 personalisation note
3. Compile in this Google Sheet template: [link]
4. Estimated time: 2-3 hours. Pay: A$30 flat.

Evaluation criteria:
- Quality of personalisation notes (not generic)
- Accuracy of data (names spelled correctly, real companies)
- Speed (how quickly delivered)
- Sheet formatting (clean, organised)
```

> **Hire the one who delivers the best personalisation notes.** Research quality is the #1 VA skill for your business.

## 6. ONBOARDING CHECKLIST

### Week 1

- [ ] Contract signed (use a simple VA agreement template — include confidentiality clause)
- [ ] Payment method set up (Wise, Payoneer, or Upwork)
- [ ] Tools access granted (Google Sheets, Notion, Calendly, Slack)
- [ ] Recorded Loom walkthrough of your business, tools, and weekly rhythm
- [ ] First task assigned: Simple, clear, with deadline. Don't start with ambiguous work.
- [ ] Daily 5-min check-in for first week (Slack or WhatsApp)

### Week 2

- [ ] Introduce slightly more complex tasks
- [ ] Give feedback on Week 1 work (both positive + corrective)
- [ ] Share your SOPs and process docs (they need to follow your system)
- [ ] Reduce check-ins to every 2-3 days

### Month 1

- [ ] Review performance: What's working? What needs improvement?
- [ ] Adjust hours up or down based on workload
- [ ] Share long-term vision: "In 6 months, I'd love you to handle X independently."

## 7. TASK DELEGATION SYSTEM

### Use a Simple Task Board (Notion, Trello, or Google Sheets)

```
Columns: Backlog → This Week → In Progress → Done → Blocked

Each task card:
  Title: Clear, verb-first ("Research 20 AU SaaS founders")
  Priority: P1 (today) / P2 (this week) / P3 (whenever)
  Deadline: Date + time
  Instructions: Step-by-step or Loom video
  Expected output: What "done" looks like
  Estimated time: So they know how long it should take
```

### Task Briefing Template

```
Task: {Clear title}
Priority: {P1/P2/P3}
Deadline: {Date, Time AEST}
Estimated time: {X} hours

What I need:
{1-3 sentences describing the task}

How to do it:
1. {Step 1}
2. {Step 2}
3. {Step 3}

Output format:
{What I expect to receive — spreadsheet, document, list?}

Example of good work:
{Link to a previous example or describe it}

Questions? Ask before starting if anything is unclear.
```

## 8. MANAGEMENT & COMMUNICATION

### Communication Cadence

| Frequency | Channel | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Daily (Week 1 only) | Slack/WhatsApp | 5-min check-in |
| 2x/week | Slack/WhatsApp | Task status, blockers |
| Weekly | Email or Loom | Priority list for next week |
| Monthly | Zoom (15 min) | Performance review, celebrations, adjustments |

### Management Principles

- **Over-communicate early.** The first month is heavy on instructions. It gets lighter.
- **Record, don't type.** A 2-min Loom video replaces 20 minutes of writing instructions.
- **Celebrate wins.** "This research list is excellent — saved me 3 hours. Thank you."
- **Don't let mistakes fester.** If something is wrong, say so immediately and kindly.
- **Trust but verify.** Spot-check their work for the first 2 months. Then reduce.
- **Pay on time, every time.** Late payments kill VA motivation faster than anything.

### Red Flags (Consider Replacing)

- Repeatedly misses deadlines without communication
- Work quality drops significantly after first month (they got comfortable)
- Doesn't ask questions when instructions are unclear (guesses instead)
- Defensive when given feedback
- Shares confidential client information

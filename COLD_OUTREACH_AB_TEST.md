# 🧪 COLD OUTREACH A/B TEST FRAMEWORK — AusAI Tech

**Purpose:** Systematically optimise your cold outreach so 100 DMs produce more clients.  
**Goal:** Double response rate (from ~5% to ~10%+) through structured testing.  
**Rule:** Never change more than one variable at a time.

> **Why this matters:** If you send 100 DMs and get 1 client (A$1,500 avg), optimizing to get 3 clients from the same 100 DMs triples your revenue with zero extra effort.

## 1. THE TESTING MINDSET

### The Golden Rule

```
Change ONE variable → Send 50+ messages → Measure → Decide → Repeat
```

**Never:** "Let me try a new hook, a new CTA, a new time, and a new platform all at once." You won't know what worked.

### Baseline First

Before testing anything, establish your baseline:

| Metric | How to Measure |
|---|---|
| **Send volume** | Total DMs/emails sent in a week |
| **Open rate** (email) | Email tracking or platform read receipts |
| **Response rate** | (Replies / Sent) × 100 |
| **Positive response rate** | (Positive replies / Sent) × 100 |
| **Meeting booked rate** | (Meetings / Sent) × 100 |
| **Close rate** | (Clients / Meetings) × 100 |
| **Cost per client** | Total time spent / Clients won |

> Track these for 2 weeks without changing anything. That's your baseline.

## 2. VARIABLES YOU CAN TEST

### Tier 1: Highest Impact (test these first)

| Variable | What to Test | Example A | Example B |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hook (first line)** | Problem statement vs question vs compliment | "I noticed your team does {X} manually" | "Curious — how much time does {X} take your team?" |
| **Offer framing** | Time saved vs money saved vs stress removed | "I save clients 15+ hrs/week" | "I help businesses cut ops costs by 40%" |
| **CTA** | "Book a call" vs "Free audit" vs "See a demo" | "Want a free 5-min Loom audit?" | "Open to a 10-min call this week?" |

### Tier 2: Medium Impact

| Variable | What to Test |
|---|---|
| **Length** | Short (3 sentences) vs Medium (5-7 sentences) vs Long (personalised paragraph) |
| **Personalisation depth** | Name + company vs Industry pain point vs Specific trigger event |
| **Time of day** | 8 AM vs 12 PM vs 4 PM vs 8 PM (AEST) |
| **Day of week** | Tuesday vs Thursday vs Saturday |
| **Platform** | LinkedIn DM vs Email vs Reddit DM vs Twitter/X DM |

### Tier 3: Lower Impact (optimise later)

| Variable | What to Test |
|---|---|
| **Subject line** (email) | Question vs number vs name vs curiosity |
| **Signature** | Title only vs Social proof ("40+ automations built") vs Link |
| **Follow-up timing** | Day 2 follow-up vs Day 5 follow-up vs No follow-up |
| **Emoji use** | No emojis vs 1-2 emojis vs emoji-heavy |

## 3. TEST CADENCE

### Weekly Testing Cycle

```
Week 1-2: BASELINE — Don't change anything. Just measure.
Week 3:   TEST 1 — Change hook only. Send 50+ DMs with new hook.
Week 4:   ANALYSE — Compare Week 3 vs baseline. Pick winner.
Week 5:   TEST 2 — Keep winning hook. Change CTA only. Send 50+ DMs.
Week 6:   ANALYSE — Compare Week 5 vs Week 3. Pick winner.
Week 7:   TEST 3 — Keep winning hook + CTA. Change offer framing.
Week 8:   ANALYSE — Pick winner.
... continue cycling through Tier 1 variables, then Tier 2, then Tier 3.
```

### Minimum Sample per Test

| Platform | Minimum Messages per Variant |
|---|---|
| LinkedIn DM | 50 (split across 2 weeks if needed) |
| Email | 100 (to get statistical meaning) |
| Reddit DM | 30 (lower volume, be patient) |
| Twitter/X DM | 30 |

**Never declare a winner with fewer than 30 messages per variant.**

## 5. WEEKLY REVIEW PROTOCOL

### Every Friday (30 min)

1. **Update spreadsheet** with this week's outreach data
2. **Calculate metrics:**
   - Response rate this week vs last week vs baseline
   - Meeting rate this week vs last week vs baseline
3. **Review individual replies:**
   - What did positive replies mention? (common phrases, objections)
   - What did negative replies say? (can you pre-empt their objection?)
4. **Decide:**
   - If ongoing test: keep running until 50+ messages
   - If test complete: pick winner, design next test
5. **Update WINNING PLAYBOOK** (§8) with any new insights

## 6. WHEN TO KILL VS DOUBLE DOWN

### Double Down (variant is working)

**Signals:**
- Response rate ≥ 2× baseline after 30+ messages
- Positive response rate higher than any previous variant
- Meetings booked > 0 (real conversion happening)

**Action:** Make this the new control. Test against it next week.

### Kill (variant isn't working)

**Signals:**
- Response rate < 50% of baseline after 40+ messages
- Zero positive responses after 50+ messages
- Negative responses outnumber positive 3:1+

**Action:** Kill the variant. Don't "give it more time." Move to next test.

### Plateau (variant is fine but not better)

**Signals:**
- Response rate within 10% of baseline after 50+ messages
- No statistical difference

**Action:** Keep the simpler version. Move to next variable.

## 7. STATISTICAL SIGNIFICANCE (QUICK)

You don't need a stats degree. Use this simplified rule:

| Difference in Response Rate | Messages per Variant | Confident? |
|---|---|---|
| 2× difference (e.g., 10% vs 5%) | 50+ | ✅ Yes |
| 1.5× difference (e.g., 7.5% vs 5%) | 100+ | ✅ Yes |
| 1.2× difference (e.g., 6% vs 5%) | 200+ | ⚠️ Maybe — more data needed |
| <1.2× difference | Any amount | ❌ Not significant — pick simpler version |

**When in doubt:** Run another 50 messages. More data is cheap.
